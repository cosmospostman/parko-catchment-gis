## Changes from V4

- **band_noise_std=0** — band jitter removed; not shown to help and adds noise (see [BAND-JITTER.md](../BAND-JITTER.md))
- **Site holdout split** — val set is entire sites (pormpuraaw + stockholm) rather than a spatial fraction; stricter generalisation test
- **Spatial stride 2** — training pixels thinned by 2× to reduce within-site spatial redundancy
- **dropout=0.75, weight_decay=0.05** — stronger regularisation
- **lr=7e-6** — lower learning rate, slower convergence
- **New training sites** — nassau ×7, pormpuraaw ×5, stockholm ×3, muttaburra ×2 added

## Assessment

**Val AUC of ~0.58 is near-chance and indicates poor generalisation to the holdout sites.**

The site holdout split (vs the prior spatial split) is a fundamentally harder test — it requires the model to transfer across geographic regions rather than just spatial subsets of the same sites. The result reveals that the model has learned features predictive within training sites but not transferable to pormpuraaw and stockholm.

Several signals point to domain shift rather than model capacity as the root cause:

- **Epoch 1 val AUC = 0.377** (below chance) — the model's initial discrimination direction is *inverted* on holdout sites, indicating the holdout sites have meaningfully different spectral/phenological character
- **Train/val AUC gap ~0.20** throughout — the model reaches 0.77–0.78 on training sites but plateaus at 0.58 on holdout, a persistent 20-point generalisation deficit
- **Val AUC improvement glacially slow after epoch 10** — from 0.572 to 0.583 over 18 epochs; the model has found its generalisation ceiling
- **Loss still falling while val AUC is flat** — late-stage overfit to training-site patterns, not model underfitting

The global feature `peak_doy=77` (mean training peak ≈ mid-March) is a candidate confounder: if pormpuraaw and stockholm have different phenological timing, the `peak_doy` and `peak_doy_cv` features may encode site identity rather than species signal. Ablating these two features is a priority diagnostic.

## Next Steps

- Ablate `peak_doy` and `peak_doy_cv` — check whether removing phenological timing features improves holdout generalisation
- Inspect pormpuraaw and stockholm `peak_doy` distributions vs training sites to confirm the timing mismatch hypothesis
- Try swapping holdout sites to something geographically closer to training to determine whether the 0.58 ceiling is pormpuraaw/stockholm-specific or a general model problem
- Consider whether `nir_cv` and `rec_p` alone (the more species-intrinsic global features) generalise better than the full global feature set
